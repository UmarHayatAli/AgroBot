# Rice / Paddy (Oryza sativa)

## Overview
Rice is Pakistan's second most important cereal crop and a major export earner. Pakistan is world-renowned for Basmati rice, primarily grown in Punjab. It is a Kharif (summer) crop heavily dependent on irrigation and monsoon rainfall. Major growing regions include Punjab (rice-wheat belt) and Sindh (Indus delta).

## Season
- **Type:** Kharif (summer crop)
- **Nursery sowing:** May – June
- **Transplanting:** June – July (after 25–30 days in nursery)
- **Harvesting:** October – November
- **Duration:** ~130–160 days

## Growing Regions in Pakistan
- **Punjab II (Rice-Wheat Belt):** Sheikhupura, Hafizabad, Gujranwala, Sialkot — Basmati heartland
- **Sindh II:** Lower Indus plain — IRRI and coarse varieties
- **Indus Delta (Zone I):** Rice, sugarcane and pulses
- **NWFP (KP) I:** Swat, Dir valleys — local varieties
- **Balochistan III:** Canal-irrigated rice in limited areas

## Ideal Soil Conditions
- **Nitrogen (N):** 75–180 kg/ha depending on region (Punjab 75–160, Sindh 134–180)
- **Phosphorus (P):** 60–100 kg/ha
- **Potassium (K):** 50–60 kg/ha (apply based on soil test)
- **Zinc:** Apply zinc sulphate (35% Zn) at 12.5 kg/ha, 7–10 days after transplanting
- **pH:** 5.5–7.0 (slightly acidic to neutral preferred)
- **Soil type:** Clayey, silty — water-holding capacity essential

## Climate Requirements
- **Temperature:** 22–35°C (optimum 25–30°C during grain filling)
- **Humidity:** 70–90% (high humidity supports growth)
- **Rainfall:** 150–300 mm during season (supplemented by canal irrigation)
- **Water requirement:** Flooded paddies in early stages, 1,200–2,000 mm total water

## Fertilizer Application Timing
- Before transplanting: all P, all K, and half of N (incorporated into mud)
- **Critical:** Do NOT apply N in standing water (volatilization loss) — apply in dry soil, then flood
- 35–40 days after transplanting (panicle initiation): remaining half of N
- Use ammoniacal nitrogen sources (urea, ammonium sulphate) — more effective for rice

## Common Diseases & Pests
- **Rice blast (Pyricularia oryzae):** Most serious fungal disease
- **Bacterial leaf blight:** Common in humid, high-N conditions
- **Brown planthopper (BPH):** Major pest, causes "hopperburn"
- **Stem borer:** Tunnels into stem, causes deadheart and whitehead
- **Sheath blight:** Fungal, favored by dense planting

## Warning Signs
- If temperature exceeds 35°C during flowering → spikelet sterility, yield drops
- If transplanting delayed past July 15 → Basmati quality deteriorates
- If zinc not applied → Khaira disease (interveinal chlorosis) in early stages
- Saline soil (ECe > 4 dS/m) → severe yield reduction

## Soil Tips
- Puddle soil before transplanting — reduces water percolation
- Level fields precisely for uniform water distribution
- Avoid sandy soils — cannot retain standing water
- Calcareous soils (common in Pakistan) → zinc deficiency, always apply ZnSO4

## Rotation Partners
- **Previous crop:** Wheat (Rabi)
- **Next crop:** Wheat (October–November sowing)
- Rice-wheat is the dominant double-cropping system in central Punjab

## Alternative Crops If Conditions Borderline
- **Maize:** If water supply is limited (rainfed option)
- **Sorghum:** If temperature is high and water scarce
- **Cotton:** If soil is well-drained and market price favorable

## Varieties Grown in Pakistan
- **Basmati:** Super Basmati, Basmati-515, Basmati-Pak (premium export)
- **IRRI/Coarse:** IRRI-6, KSK-282 (high yield, domestic consumption)
- **Fine non-Basmati:** PK-386, Shua-92

## Market & Economic Notes
- Major export crop — Pakistan earns $2+ billion annually from rice exports
- Basmati commands 3–5x premium price over coarse varieties
- Middle East, Europe, and Africa are primary export destinations
- Government supports procurement through TCP (Trading Corporation of Pakistan)
