# Wheat (Triticum aestivum)

## Overview
Wheat is Pakistan's most important staple food crop, contributing 10.3% to agricultural value added and 2.2% to GDP. It is the primary diet of the population and a central crop in national agricultural policy. Pakistan cultivates wheat on approximately 9.1 million hectares annually, producing around 26–27 million metric tons. Punjab contributes over 75% of national production.

## Season
- **Type:** Rabi (winter crop)
- **Sowing:** November (first two weeks preferred) – December
- **Harvesting:** April – May
- **Duration:** ~150–160 days

## Growing Regions in Pakistan
- **Punjab I, II, III:** Primary irrigated wheat belt (cotton-wheat, rice-wheat rotations)
- **Punjab IV, V:** Rainfed wheat on Potwar Plateau and Salt Range
- **Sindh I, II:** Canal-irrigated wheat in lower Indus plain
- **NWFP (KP) I, II:** Maize-wheat and mixed cropping systems
- **Balochistan II, III:** Limited rainfed and canal-irrigated areas

## Ideal Soil Conditions
- **Nitrogen (N):** 75–170 kg/ha (irrigated); 60–100 kg/ha (rainfed)
- **Phosphorus (P):** 60–110 kg/ha (irrigated); 30–75 kg/ha (rainfed)
- **Potassium (K):** 50–60 kg/ha (irrigated); minimal for rainfed
- **pH:** 6.5–8.0 (tolerates slightly alkaline soils common in Pakistan)
- **Soil type:** Loam, clay-loam, silty clay — holds moisture well

## Climate Requirements
- **Temperature:** 15–25°C during growing season; needs cool winter for tillering
- **Germination temp:** 20–25°C optimal
- **Humidity:** Moderate (40–60%)
- **Rainfall:** 250–500 mm per season (irrigation supplements in arid zones)
- **Water requirement:** ~400mm total (16 inches/acre traditional measure)

## Fertilizer Application Timing
- At sowing: all P, all K, and half of N (broadcast and incorporated)
- First or second irrigation: remaining half of N (top dressing)
- Light soils: split N into three applications
- Rainfed areas: apply all fertilizer at sowing

## Common Diseases & Pests
- **Yellow rust (stripe rust):** Most damaging fungal disease in Pakistan
- **Brown rust (leaf rust):** Common in Punjab
- **Loose smut:** Seed-borne disease
- **Aphids:** Major pest during grain filling
- **Karnal bunt:** Fungal disease affecting grain quality

## Warning Signs
- If sown after December 15 → yield drops 1–1.5% per day of delay
- If pH exceeds 8.5 → iron and zinc deficiency likely, apply micronutrients
- If humidity stays above 80% during heading → rust risk increases sharply
- Waterlogged soil → root rot, yield loss of 20–40%

## Soil Tips
- 3–4 ploughings and planking for good seedbed preparation
- Level land well for uniform water distribution
- Apply zinc sulphate if soil Zn is below 0.5 mg/kg
- Avoid waterlogging — drainage essential on clay soils

## Rotation Partners
- **Previous crop:** Cotton, rice, maize, sugarcane
- **Next crop:** Cotton (Kharif), rice (Kharif), maize
- Legume before wheat → reduce N application by 20–30 kg/ha

## Alternative Crops If Conditions Borderline
- **Barley:** If rainfall is very low (<250mm) or soil is more saline
- **Chickpea:** If soil is lighter and rainfall unreliable
- **Mustard/Canola:** If farm is smaller and oil crop preferred

## Market & Economic Notes
- Government sets Minimum Support Price (MSP) annually
- Procurement by PASSCO and provincial food departments
- High domestic demand — Pakistan still imports wheat in deficit years
- Rust-resistant varieties promoted: Akbar-19, Pakistan-2013, AARI-2011
