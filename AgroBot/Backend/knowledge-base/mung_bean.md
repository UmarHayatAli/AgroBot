# Mung Bean / Green Gram (Vigna radiata)

## Overview
Mung bean (moong) is a short-duration, warm-season pulse crop that fits excellently into Pakistan's cropping systems as an inter-crop or catch crop. It is an important source of dietary protein, especially in rural areas. Its short duration (60–70 days) makes it ideal between Rabi and Kharif seasons or as a relay crop in cotton fields.

## Season
- **Type:** Kharif (warm season) — also grown as Zaid (spring/inter-season)
- **Spring/Zaid sowing:** March – April (between Rabi harvest and Kharif planting)
- **Kharif sowing:** June – July
- **Harvesting:** May – June (spring); August – September (Kharif)
- **Duration:** 60–75 days (very short)

## Growing Regions in Pakistan
- **Punjab:** Widespread — Jhang, Faisalabad, Gujranwala, Sargodha; relay crop in cotton fields
- **Sindh III:** Mixed crop areas
- **NWFP (KP) III:** Pulses belt
- **Balochistan:** Limited rainfed cultivation

## Ideal Soil Conditions
- **Nitrogen (N):** 20–30 kg/ha (low — nitrogen fixing legume; starter dose only)
- **Phosphorus (P):** 40–60 kg/ha
- **Potassium (K):** 20–30 kg/ha where deficient
- **Rhizobium inoculation:** Bradyrhizobium seed inoculation recommended
- **pH:** 6.0–7.5
- **Soil type:** Sandy loam to loam — good drainage essential; cannot tolerate waterlogging

## Climate Requirements
- **Temperature:** 25–35°C (warm crop — frost kills immediately)
- **Humidity:** 40–70%
- **Rainfall:** 300–500 mm; very drought tolerant for a pulse
- **Critical:** Requires dry weather at podding and harvest — rain shatters pods

## Fertilizer Application Timing
- All fertilizer at sowing (short crop duration, no splits needed)
- Rhizobium inoculation of seed before planting

## Common Diseases & Pests
- **Yellow mosaic virus (Mung bean yellow mosaic virus — MYMV):** Most serious; spread by whitefly; causes complete crop failure
- **Cercospora leaf spot:** Brown circular spots on leaves
- **Powdery mildew:** In cool, humid conditions
- **Pod borer (Maruca vitrata):** Attacks flowers and pods
- **Whitefly:** Primary MYMV vector — manage with imidacloprid

## Warning Signs
- Yellow mosaic on young leaves → MYMV, uproot and destroy infected plants immediately
- Rain during podding → pod shattering, harvest immediately (early morning)
- Waterlogging for >24 hours → complete root rot

## Soil Tips
- Short duration makes it an excellent soil health crop — fixes N, adds organic matter
- Does not require deep tillage — 2 ploughings sufficient
- Can be grown on residual moisture after wheat harvest (spring crop)

## Rotation Partners
- **Spring:** After wheat harvest → before Kharif cotton or rice
- **Kharif:** Relay in cotton fields (intercrop between cotton rows)
- Nitrogen fixed (40–80 kg N/ha) benefits the next wheat or cotton crop

## Alternative Crops If Conditions Borderline
- **Sesame (Til):** If temperature is very high and soil is light
- **Moth bean:** If conditions are drier and soil sandier
- **Cowpea:** If humidity is higher and temperature hotter

## Market & Economic Notes
- Strong domestic demand — moong dal is a staple in Pakistani cuisine
- Price premium for large-seeded bold varieties
- Export to Middle East and South Asian markets
- Short duration makes it low-risk for farmers — quick cash return
- Government promotes mung bean to improve dietary diversity and pulse self-sufficiency
