# Mango (Mangifera indica)

## Overview
Pakistan is the world's fourth largest mango producer, with orchards concentrated in Sindh and Punjab. Mango is the national fruit and a major export crop. The Sindhri variety from Sindh and Chaunsa from Punjab are globally recognized premium varieties. Mango orchards are a long-term investment — trees bear fruit for 30–50 years.

## Season
- **Type:** Perennial tree crop; fruits in summer
- **Flowering:** February – March
- **Fruit development:** March – June
- **Harvesting:** May – August (variety dependent)
- **Tree bearing:** 4–5 years after planting; full production at 10+ years

## Growing Regions in Pakistan
- **Sindh:** Mirpurkhas, Tando Allah Yar, Hyderabad — Sindhri heartland
- **Punjab:** Multan, Muzaffargarh, Rahim Yar Khan — Chaunsa, Langra, Anwar Ratol
- **Zone IVa (Northern Irrigated Plain):** Canal-irrigated mango belt

## Ideal Soil & Nutritional Conditions
- **Nitrogen (N):** Young tree (year 1): 150g/plant, increasing 75g/year; mature tree (10yr): ~1.5 kg/plant
- **Phosphorus (P):** Year 1: 50g/plant, increasing 50g/year; mature tree: ~0.5 kg/plant
- **Potassium (K):** Year 1: 50g/plant, increasing 25g/year; mature tree: ~0.5 kg/plant
- **Farm Yard Manure:** 10–15 kg/plant (young); 50 kg/plant (mature)
- **Micronutrients:** Zinc, boron, iron — foliar sprays recommended annually
- **pH:** 5.5–7.5 (slightly acidic to neutral preferred)
- **Soil type:** Deep, well-drained loam or sandy loam (2+ meters depth for roots)

## Climate Requirements
- **Temperature:** 24–42°C growing season; needs cool (10–15°C) dry winter for flowering induction
- **Humidity:** 50–75% during growing season; low humidity at flowering critical
- **Rainfall:** 750–2,500 mm (irrigated in Pakistan — 6–8 irrigations/year)
- **Critical:** Dry, cool weather from November to February → triggers flower bud differentiation
- **Rain at flowering (Feb–Mar):** Destroys pollination → complete crop failure

## Fertilizer Application Timing
- Apply fertilizer twice yearly: post-harvest (August–September) and pre-flowering (October–November)
- Mix NPK with FYM and apply in ring around drip line of tree
- Foliar spray of micronutrients at bud burst and fruit development stages

## Common Diseases & Pests
- **Mango malformation:** Most serious disease in Pakistan — deforms flowers and vegetative shoots; no cure, prune and destroy
- **Anthracnose (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides):** Black spots on fruit — favored by humid conditions
- **Powdery mildew:** White powder on flowers — destroys flowering in humid conditions
- **Mango mealybug:** Sap-sucking pest, causes sooty mold
- **Mango fruit fly (Bactrocera dorsalis):** Maggots inside fruit — biggest export barrier
- **Hoppers (Amritodus atkinsoni):** Suck sap from flowers and shoots

## Warning Signs
- Flower emergence in November (too early) → hopper damage, spray imidacloprid
- Rain at pollination → antifungal spray immediately (mancozeb, carbendazim)
- Fruit dropping at marble size → water stress or calcium deficiency
- Black spots on mature fruit → anthracnose, treat post-harvest with hot water (52°C, 5 min)

## Orchard Management Tips
- Spacing: 10m × 10m (100 trees/hectare) for standard varieties
- Pruning after harvest removes crowded and diseased branches, improves aeration
- Intercropping in young orchards: vegetables, legumes (for first 4 years)
- Irrigation: critical at fruit set (April) and sizing (May–June); stop 2 weeks before harvest

## Varieties Grown in Pakistan
- **Sindhri:** Pride of Sindh; large, sweet, export quality; harvests June–July
- **Chaunsa:** Punjab's premium variety; excellent flavor, late season (July–August)
- **Langra:** Old variety; unique resinous flavor; June–July
- **Anwar Ratol:** Small, intensely sweet; premium market; July
- **Dussehri:** Early variety; high yield; May–June

## Alternative If Conditions Not Suitable
- **Citrus (kinnow):** If soil is slightly saline or heavier clay
- **Guava:** More tolerant of poor soils and less water
- **Date palm:** If conditions are extremely hot and dry (Sindh interior)

## Market & Economic Notes
- Pakistan exports 100,000–150,000 tons of mango annually
- Fruit fly infestation is the primary barrier to EU and US market access
- Irradiation treatment facilities being expanded for premium market access
- Sindhri commands highest export price ($1.5–2.5/kg FOB)
- Domestic market is largest consumer — urban demand growing rapidly
