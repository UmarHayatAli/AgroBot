# Maize / Corn (Zea mays)

## Overview
Maize is one of Pakistan's fastest-growing crops, driven by rising demand from the poultry feed and silage industry. In 2024, over 1.6 million acres were under maize cultivation, mostly in Punjab and KP. Hybrid varieties have pushed average yields above 80 maunds/acre. It is grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons in suitable areas.

## Season
- **Type:** Primarily Kharif (summer); some Rabi in KP/northern areas
- **Kharif sowing:** April – June
- **Rabi sowing (KP/north):** October – November
- **Harvesting:** August – October (Kharif); February – March (Rabi)
- **Duration:** ~90–120 days

## Growing Regions in Pakistan
- **NWFP (KP) I (Maize-Wheat Belt):** Primary maize zone — Swat, Dir, Buner, Mardan (rainfed + irrigated)
- **Punjab V (Maize-Wheat-Oilseeds):** Rainfed uplands — Rawalpindi, Chakwal, Jhelum
- **Punjab (irrigated plains):** Faisalabad, Okara — hybrid commercial maize
- **Balochistan:** Limited irrigated areas

## Ideal Soil Conditions
- **Nitrogen (N):** 90–120 kg/ha (KP irrigated); 60–90 kg/ha (Punjab rainfed)
- **Phosphorus (P):** 60–90 kg/ha
- **Potassium (K):** 30–60 kg/ha (where needed by soil test)
- **pH:** 5.5–7.5 (slightly acidic to neutral)
- **Soil type:** Well-drained loam or sandy loam — does NOT tolerate waterlogging

## Climate Requirements
- **Temperature:** 18–32°C optimum; below 10°C stops growth
- **Humidity:** 50–80%
- **Rainfall:** 600–900 mm (rainfed KP); 400–600 mm irrigated areas
- **Water requirement:** Critical at tasseling, silking, and grain filling stages
- **Drought sensitive** at pollination — yield collapses if water stressed then

## Fertilizer Application Timing
- At sowing: all P, all K, and 1/3 of N
- At knee-high stage (~30 days): 1/3 of N
- At tasseling (~60 days): remaining 1/3 of N
- Zinc application beneficial in deficient soils

## Common Diseases & Pests
- **Maize stem borer (Chilo partellus):** Most serious pest in Pakistan — bores into stem
- **Fall armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda):** Invasive pest, rapidly spreading in Pakistan
- **Northern corn leaf blight:** Fungal, large tan lesions on leaves
- **Corn smut (Ustilago maydis):** Galls on ears
- **Cutworms:** Damage seedlings at soil level

## Warning Signs
- If temperature exceeds 35°C at silking → poor pollination, barren cobs
- Yellowing starting from leaf tip → nitrogen deficiency
- Purple coloration of leaves → phosphorus deficiency (common in cold, wet conditions)
- Lodging (plant falls over) → K deficiency or excessive N without K balance

## Soil Tips
- Deep tillage (30–35 cm) for good root development
- Well-drained fields essential — maize dies within 48–72 hours of waterlogging
- Organic matter addition improves yield significantly in KP rainfed areas
- Avoid planting in low-lying fields prone to flooding

## Rotation Partners
- **KP:** Maize–wheat is the dominant rotation
- **Punjab rainfed:** Maize–wheat–oilseeds
- Maize residue (stover) used for silage or as soil organic matter

## Uses of Maize in Pakistan
- **Poultry feed:** 70%+ of production goes to poultry industry
- **Silage:** Dairy farmers in Punjab use whole-plant silage
- **Human food:** Corn flour (makki ki roti) — staple in KP and rural Punjab
- **Industrial:** Starch, glucose, ethanol

## Alternative Crops If Conditions Borderline
- **Sorghum:** If rainfall is less or temperature higher
- **Millet (Bajra):** If soil is sandy and water very scarce
- **Sunflower:** If early Kharif season and medium water availability

## Market & Economic Notes
- Poultry industry drives demand — prices follow poultry feed market
- Hybrid seed adoption rapidly increasing — PIONEER, Monsanto hybrids dominant
- Yield gap is large — farmer average 3–4 tons/ha vs potential 8–10 tons/ha
- KP's rainfed maize is lower yield but lower input cost
- Government promoting maize to reduce wheat area in water-scarce zones
