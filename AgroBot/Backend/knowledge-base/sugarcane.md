# Sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum)

## Overview
Sugarcane is Pakistan's most important industrial crop and the primary source of sugar. It is a long-duration, high water-consuming crop grown in both Punjab and Sindh. Pakistan's sugar industry depends entirely on domestic sugarcane. It also provides by-products including molasses (for ethanol), bagasse (for paper/fuel), and press mud (fertilizer).

## Season
- **Type:** Kharif (planted in spring/autumn, harvested the following year)
- **Spring planting:** February – March (most common)
- **Autumn planting:** September – October
- **Harvesting:** November – March (12–14 months after spring planting)
- **Duration:** 12–18 months

## Growing Regions in Pakistan
- **Punjab III (Mixed Crops):** Faisalabad, Sargodha, Jhang, Gujranwala — major cane belt
- **Sindh III (Mixed Crops):** Nawabshah, Mirpurkhas, Hyderabad
- **NWFP (KP) II:** Peshawar, Mardan valleys (high yield per acre due to climate)
- **Indus Delta (Zone I):** Limited cultivation

## Ideal Soil Conditions
- **Nitrogen (N):** 170–270 kg/ha (Punjab); 200–300 kg/ha (Sindh); 120–175 kg/ha (KP)
- **Phosphorus (P):** 60–125 kg/ha depending on region
- **Potassium (K):** 60–170 kg/ha (high K demand — important for sugar content)
- **Farm Yard Manure:** 20–25 cart loads/ha at soil preparation (1 month before planting)
- **pH:** 6.0–7.5 (slightly acidic to neutral preferred)
- **Soil type:** Deep, well-drained loam or clay loam

## Climate Requirements
- **Temperature:** 20–40°C; optimum 27–35°C for growth; 15–20°C for ripening (increases sucrose)
- **Humidity:** 60–80%
- **Rainfall:** 1,500–2,500 mm total (heavily irrigated in Pakistan — 8–10 irrigations/season)
- **Cool dry weather at maturity** → increases sugar content significantly
- **Frost:** Kills the crop — avoid in frost-prone northern areas

## Fertilizer Application Timing
- At planting: all P, all K, and 1/3 of N in furrows below seed sets (avoid direct seed contact)
- April: 1/3 of N (top dressing)
- May: remaining 1/3 of N
- Ratoon crop: 30% more fertilizer than newly planted crop
- Ratoon N application: half at sprouting (with P and K), half 2 months later

## Common Diseases & Pests
- **Red rot (Colletotrichum falcatum):** Most serious fungal disease — red discoloration inside stalk
- **Smut (Sporisorium scitamineum):** Whip-like black growth from growing point
- **Pyrilla (sugarcane leafhopper):** Major sucking pest, excretes honeydew → sooty mold
- **Stem borer (Chilo infuscatellus):** Deadheart in early stage, yield loss up to 30%
- **Grassy shoot disease:** Phytoplasma — turns shoots grassy, no cure, rogue out

## Warning Signs
- If ratoon crop shows poor sprouting → soil compaction, apply earthing up
- Yellowing of lower leaves → nitrogen deficiency, top dress immediately
- Red discoloration inside stalks → red rot, destroy affected stools
- Waterlogging for >3 days → root suffocation, yield loss severe

## Soil Tips
- Deep ploughing (45–60 cm) before planting essential
- Earthing up at 2–3 months improves anchorage and ratoon yield
- Avoid replanting ratoon beyond 3–4 years — disease buildup
- Subsoil drainage critical in Sindh heavy soils

## Ratoon Management
- Ratoon (regrowth after first harvest) — 1–3 ratoons common
- Ratoon needs 30% more fertilizer
- Trash mulching after harvest conserves moisture and suppresses weeds

## Rotation Partners
- **Previous crop:** Wheat, legumes, vegetables
- **Next crop:** Wheat (when sugarcane cycle ends)
- Long duration limits flexibility in crop rotation

## Alternative Crops If Conditions Borderline
- **Sugar beet:** If climate is cooler and water limited (emerging crop in KP)
- **Maize:** If planting window is missed
- **Sorghum:** If water supply is unreliable

## Market & Economic Notes
- Pakistan has 80+ sugar mills, mostly in Punjab and Sindh
- Government regulates ex-mill price and support price for growers
- Molasses used for ethanol production — growing industrial demand
- KP sugarcane commands higher sugar recovery (13–14% vs 8–10% Punjab)
- Chronic issue: delayed payments from sugar mills to farmers
