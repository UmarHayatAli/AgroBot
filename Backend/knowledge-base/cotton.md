# Cotton (Gossypium hirsutum)

## Overview
Cotton is Pakistan's most important cash crop, known as "white gold." It is the backbone of Pakistan's textile industry, which contributes 60% of export earnings. Cotton is grown primarily in Punjab and Sindh under irrigated conditions. Pakistan is among the world's top cotton producers.

## Season
- **Type:** Kharif (summer crop)
- **Sowing:** Late April – May (Punjab); March – April (Sindh)
- **Harvesting:** September – November (multiple pickings)
- **Duration:** ~180–200 days

## Growing Regions in Pakistan
- **Punjab I (Cotton-Wheat Belt):** Multan, Bahawalpur, Rahim Yar Khan, Vehari — largest area
- **Sindh I:** Sanghar, Mirpurkhas, Nawabshah — second major zone
- **Balochistan:** Limited irrigated areas

## Ideal Soil Conditions
- **Nitrogen (N):** 120–170 kg/ha (Punjab); 90–140 kg/ha (Sindh)
- **Phosphorus (P):** 60 kg/ha
- **Potassium (K):** 50–60 kg/ha (based on soil test)
- **Boron:** Critical micronutrient — apply carefully (narrow deficiency-toxicity range)
- **pH:** 6.0–8.0 (tolerates slight alkalinity)
- **Soil type:** Sandy loam to clay loam — well-drained essential

## Climate Requirements
- **Temperature:** 27–35°C optimum; germination needs >18°C
- **Humidity:** 40–70% (low to moderate — excessive humidity promotes disease)
- **Rainfall:** 500–800 mm (mostly irrigation-dependent in Pakistan)
- **Frost sensitive:** Any frost destroys the crop
- **Water requirement:** Critical at flowering and boll formation stages

## Fertilizer Application Timing
- At sowing: all P, all K, and 1/3 of N (band placement in furrows below seeds)
- First irrigation: 1/3 of N
- Pre-flowering stage: remaining 1/3 of N
- Apply 5–10 tons/acre farm yard manure (FYM) at last ploughing (rainfed); 10–20 tons (irrigated)
- Boron application based on soil test — foliar spray if deficient

## Common Diseases & Pests
- **Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCuV):** Most devastating disease in Pakistan — spread by whitefly
- **Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci):** Primary pest and CLCuV vector
- **Pink bollworm:** Damages bolls; major yield loss
- **American bollworm:** Polyphagous pest, attacks bolls
- **Bacterial blight (Angular leaf spot):** Seed-borne, favored by humid conditions
- **Fusarium wilt:** Soil-borne fungal disease

## Warning Signs
- Leaf curling, yellowing, vein darkening → Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (no cure, remove affected plants)
- If humidity exceeds 80% during boll formation → boll rot risk
- Temperature above 40°C during flowering → boll shedding increases
- Continuous cotton in same field → buildup of soil-borne pathogens (rotate crops)

## Soil Tips
- Avoid continuous cotton cultivation in the same field
- Deep ploughing (30–40 cm) breaks hardpan and improves drainage
- Potassium humate (5–10 liters/acre) after 60 days of sowing improves growth
- Well-drained soils critical — waterlogging kills cotton within days

## Topping Practice
- Cut the growing point of the main branch at ~5 feet height
- Encourages lateral branching → more boll-bearing branches → higher yield

## Rotation Partners
- **Previous crop:** Wheat (excellent rotation)
- **Next crop:** Wheat (Rabi season)
- Cotton-wheat is the dominant rotation in southern Punjab and Sindh

## Alternative Crops If Conditions Borderline
- **Sunflower:** If season is shorter or temperatures moderate
- **Sorghum:** If water is scarce
- **Maize:** If soil is heavier and early Kharif season

## Market & Economic Notes
- Directly linked to Pakistan's $20+ billion textile export industry
- Price fluctuates with international cotton market
- CLCuV remains the biggest threat to Pakistan's cotton production
- Government promotes Bt cotton varieties for bollworm resistance
- Multan, Bahawalpur corridor is the premium cotton-producing zone
